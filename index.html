The story so far

I didn't set out to be a machine learning engineer. I started as a curious kid who stayed up too late watching neural networks learn to play video games on YouTube. Eventually that curiosity found its way into a mathematics degree, a few research papers, and a career spent at the intersection of statistics and software.

Over the past six years I've built recommendation engines that serve millions of users, fine-tuned language models for domain-specific tasks, and coached teams on how to deploy ML reliably in production — not just in notebooks. I care deeply about the gap between "it works on my laptop" and "it works at scale."

Outside of work you'll find me hiking, making pour-over coffee with a spreadsheet of brew variables, or mentoring junior engineers who remind me why this field is so exciting.
